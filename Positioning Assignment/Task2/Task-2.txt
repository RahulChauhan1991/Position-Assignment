Relative Positioning (position: relative;):
a)An element with relative positioning is positioned relative to its normal position.
b)Setting the top, right, bottom, and left properties of a relatively-positioned element will cause it to adjust from its normal position.
c)Other content will not be adjusted to fit into any gap left by the element.

2)Absolute Positioning (position: absolute;):

a)An element with absolute positioning is positioned relative to the nearest positioned ancestor.
b)If no positioned ancestor is present, then it uses the document body as the parent.
c)Absolute positioned elements are removed from the normal flow, and can overlap other elements.
